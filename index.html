Everything you need to know about the Whitney High School Veteran and Military Appreciation Club.

Our Mission Statement

[filler text]

Purpose of the Club

  • To express gratitude and appreciation for the service and sacrifices of active U.S. military and veterans.
  • To provide support and guidance for high school students interested in enlisting or becoming officers in the United States military.
  • To educate students about military history, service opportunities, and the importance of civic duty.
  • To build community through collaboration with local veterans’ organizations and military-focused charities.
  • To help students develop leadership skills, physical fitness, and the mental resilience needed for military service.

Membership (Article III)

  • Open to all students of Whitney High School interested in the U.S. military, veterans, and leadership development.
  • Members are expected to uphold respect, gratitude, and teamwork while participating in Club activities.
  • Members are encouraged to attend meetings, participate in events, and engage in volunteer opportunities.

Club Officers and Leadership (Article IV)

  • President: Oversees activities and direction, leads meetings, represents Club.
  • Vice President: Assists the President, steps in when needed.
  • Secretary: Keeps meeting records, tracks attendance, maintains communication.
  • Treasurer: Manages finances, fundraising, and donations.
  • Public Relations Officer: Promotes Club events and activities.

Club Activities (Article V)

Community Service and Fundraisers

  • Participate in charity events supporting military-related causes.
  • Host clothing and food drives for homeless veterans.
  • Partner with local veterans’ organizations for community efforts and events.

Weekend Field Trips

  • Visit military sites such as the U.S. Battleship in San Pedro, March Air Base, Los Alamitos Air Base, and local airshows.
  • Learn about military history and aircraft firsthand.

Guest Speakers

  • Invite military academy/ROTC liaison officers and community veterans or military historians to speak at meetings.
  • Host webinars or talks with former Whitney students serving in ROTC/Service Academies.

Book or Movie Watching and Analysis

  • Select military-themed books or movies and hold group discussions on leadership, values, and veteran experiences.

Physical Fitness Training Days

  • Organize fitness days to meet military service physical standards.
  • Encourage teamwork and healthy competition.

Meetings (Article VI)

  • Monthly meetings during lunch or after school, unless otherwise specified.
  • Special meetings for events, service projects, or guest speakers as needed.
  • President leads meetings with officer assistance.
  • Members notified via email, social media, or school bulletin.

Finances and Community Service Events (Article VII)

  • No cash funds raised; organize collection drives to support troops and veterans.
  • Treasurer and Public Relations Officer coordinate collection initiatives to support Club goals and partnered organizations.

Non-Discrimination (Article IX)

The Whitney High School VMAC is committed to inclusivity and does not discriminate based on race, color, national origin, gender, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic. All students are welcome.

Adopted by the Whitney High School Veteran and Military Appreciation Club on August 8, 2025. This document outlines the founding principles and activities of the Club, ensuring a positive impact on both veterans and students.
